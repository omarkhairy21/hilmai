# Financial Health Score Calculator

## Overview
Your Financial Health Score is a comprehensive assessment of your overall financial well-being. It evaluates multiple aspects of your finances to provide a score from 0-100 and personalized recommendations for improvement.

## What Gets Measured
- **Emergency Fund**: Do you have 3-6 months of expenses saved?
- **Debt Management**: What's your debt-to-income ratio?
- **Savings Rate**: What percentage of income do you save?
- **Credit Health**: Do you have good credit habits?
- **Insurance Coverage**: Are you adequately protected?
- **Retirement Planning**: Are you saving for the future?
- **Spending Control**: Do you track and control expenses?

## Score Ranges

### Excellent (80-100)
- Strong financial foundation
- Well-prepared for emergencies
- On track for long-term goals
- Minimal financial stress

### Good (60-79)
- Solid financial position
- Some areas for improvement
- Generally on the right track
- Room to optimize

### Fair (40-59)
- Basic financial stability
- Several areas need attention
- Some financial stress
- Action needed

### Needs Improvement (20-39)
- Financial challenges present
- Multiple areas need work
- Significant stress likely
- Urgent action required

### Critical (0-19)
- Serious financial difficulties
- Immediate intervention needed
- High financial stress
- Professional help may be needed

## How to Improve Your Score

### Emergency Fund
- Build 3-6 months of expenses
- Keep in high-yield savings
- Don't touch except emergencies

### Debt Management
- Pay off high-interest debt first
- Keep DTI below 36%
- Avoid new debt

### Savings Rate
- Aim for 20% of income
- Automate savings
- Increase gradually

### Credit Health
- Pay bills on time
- Keep credit utilization low
- Don't close old accounts
- Check credit reports regularly

### Insurance
- Health insurance coverage
- Adequate life insurance
- Disability insurance if needed
- Property insurance

### Retirement
- Contribute to 401(k) or IRA
- Take employer match if available
- Increase contributions over time

### Spending Control
- Track all expenses
- Create and follow a budget
- Review spending regularly
- Cut unnecessary expenses

## Common Questions

**How accurate is this score?**
The score is a general assessment based on common financial principles. Your personal situation may vary.

**What if my score is low?**
Don't panic! Focus on one area at a time. Start with building an emergency fund, then tackle debt.

**How often should I check my score?**
Review quarterly or when your financial situation changes significantly.

**Does this affect my credit score?**
No, this is separate from your credit score. It's a broader assessment of overall financial health.

**Can I improve quickly?**
Some improvements take time (like building savings), but you can start immediately. Small consistent actions lead to big changes.
